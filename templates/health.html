Mental Health

How can I look after my mental health while at uni?

Nurture your wellbeing by following the Five Steps to Wellbeing:

  • Connect with Others
  • Keep Active
  • Keep Learning
  • Help Others
  • Practice Mindfulness

The University offers free online CBT sessions via Silvercloud.
For more information on support and resources available please click here.
If you need support, please don't hesitate to contact Student Welfare who will be able to offer you support and guidance.

Register With a GP and Dentist

How can I find a doctor or dentist in Newcastle?

It is essential you look after your health while at uni and make sure you have regular check ups with your dentist and visit your GP when needed.
You can register with a GP here: https://www.studentgpreg.org.uk/ You can find local Dentist surgeries here: https://www.nhs.uk/Service-Search/find-a-dentist

Sexual Health

How can I look after my sexual health while at uni?

Newcastle Sexual Health Service provides a variety of free support offering:

  • contraception
  • testing for sexually transmitted infections (STIs or STDs)
  • information on abortion services
  • walk-in clinics
  • booked appointments

Free condoms, lubricant, home chlamydia testing kits and emergency sanitary products can be picked up from the Student Advice Centre.

PEC Forms

What is a Pec Form?

If an unforseen personal circumstance has affected your ability to study, you should fill in a Personal Extenuating Circumstances (PEC) Form in order to request appropriate adjustments to be put in place. Information on how to fill in the form can be found on the next card.
